We built 4ward because we needed it ourselves.

I'm Marley, a junior, and honestly I still don't have the clearest picture of what I want to do after college. I have interests and ideas, but turning those into a real direction is hard.

What I did figure out is that the people who could help most, my advisors, were the ones I struggled to use well. I'd book a meeting, sit down, and not really know what to ask, then leave with a few notes but not much more clarity.

As an athlete, I always had a coach breaking things down, a game plan, and honest feedback on what to work on next. Careers never felt like that. So Alex and I started building the thing we wished we had.

4ward isn't here to tell you exactly who to become, and it won't replace your advisor. It gives you an honest read on where you're headed, shows you what actually moves the needle, and helps you walk into those conversations with better questions and a real sense of direction.

We're still students figuring it out too. That's exactly why we're building 4ward, so the next conversation you have about your future is a better one.
